A Tutorial - Easy Meals for a Student

This is my tutorial page, wherein I shall place information pertaining to the creation of three simple and fairly cheap meals to carry a student throughout the day - these are not, however, at all definitive, and I recommend you find at least variations upon the given options.



Ready? Go!

Breakfast - Cereal

In this image we can see the basic requirements for our first meal - cereal. Firstly, we have the most important part, a bowl - after all, we need to put it somewhere . Then, of course, we have the dry cereal itself, and the revitalisng, nourishing milk. Mmmm.

In this image, you can see the combination of the three, with the dry cereal already inside the bowl, and the milk being poured in, by the lovely me. Now, you should be careful here - don't fill the bowl much more than halfway with cereal, and never do more than enough milk that the top layer begins to float. Naturally, keep the overall level a centimetre or so below the rim of your crockery, unless you really like cleaning. Now, just grab a spoon and dig in!

 

Lunch- Pitta Bread

This second meal is specifically for lunch, though like all these meals you can theoretically have it at any time - these are arranged in an order dictated by common sense, but feel free to ignore it. What we want to make is effectively a pitta bread sandwich. We require the pitta, obviously, a knife for cutting and spreading, as well as whatever you wish to put in the pitta bread - in my case, a nice spread and some pork luncheon meat.

You will need to toast the pitta until it is on the verge of crispiness, and then spread the spread evenly spread across the inside of the pitta. Then, add any other filling. Finally, it should end up vaguely similar to the image on the right - I suggest you eat it at this point.

 

Tea - Pasta Stuff

Our final meal in this series is slightly more complicated, but has far more opportunities for your own preferred variation, being a pasta dish. Here you can see the ingredients we shall be using - pasta (which can be any type), sausages, cheese, and a sauce - here I have used a korma curry sauce, because it's what I had available. Again, this is a dish that can easily be adjusted to your taste. Keep in mind, however, that the quantities here are for 2 very hungry people.

Now, first thing you're going to want to do, is cook your sausages - this will usually only be about twenty minutes - similar to pasta cooking time. Very convenient. As such, you will want to start cooking your pasta at the same time. Once the sausages are cooked, you should remove them from the oven (and turn it off) and chop them into rough quarters. Your pasta should now be cooked, or close to. Wait if needed, then drain it.

Finally, tip the sauce, cheese, sausages and any other stuff you want into the pasta. Mix. Vigorously. Keep it on the heat for a few seconds to even more thoroughly burn melt your cheese. Once all mixed, however, it is effectively done. At this point, you can decant it into a dish of your choosing and eat it. Then again, you can add extras, such as any herbs and spices you have. Personally, we also added an egg on the top, which worked surprisingly well. Anyhow, good luck, and try not to give yourself food poisoning.
